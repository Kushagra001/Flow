# PRODUCT.md — Flōw

## Product Name
Flōw

## Tagline
Think less. Build more.

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
AI productivity SaaS that turns messy voice notes, browser tabs, documents, and screenshots into structured, prioritised task lists — automatically. No manual organisation required.

## Users
- **Primary:** Indie hackers and startup founders overwhelmed by context-switching and information overload
- **Secondary:** Product managers at early-stage companies who live in too many tools
- **Pain:** They capture ideas everywhere but never process them; they switch tools 40+ times a day; their "second brain" is actually a graveyard

## Core Promise
Everything you throw at Flōw gets converted to clear, actionable tasks automatically — before you even think to organise it.

## Tone
- Confident, slightly premium, not corporate
- Direct: short sentences, no hedging
- Slightly irreverent — aware of the problem from the inside
- Like Linear meets Notion: opinionated, clean, purposeful
- No buzzwords. No "revolutionise", "supercharge", "seamless"

## Visual Identity
- Light, open, and optimistic — warm cream/off-white surfaces bathed in sunlight, with bold vermilion accents
- Inspired by: Editorial magazine design (Kinfolk, Monocle), high-end architecture portfolios, optimistic consumer brands
- NOT: overly playful/colorful, startup-beige SaaS, tech-bro dark mode, Vercel/Linear clones
- Premium without being clinical. Warm, airy, and inviting.

## Color Strategy
Committed — a bold warm vermilion/terracotta carries the brand identity. Set against warm cream surfaces.
- Brand vermilion: #ea580c / oklch(59.9% 0.177 39)
- Vermilion hover/glow: #f97316 / oklch(65.1% 0.187 43)
- Base surface (warm cream): #faf7f2 / oklch(97% 0.005 80)
- Secondary surface: #f3efea / oklch(95% 0.006 80)
- Text (warm charcoal): #292524 / oklch(25% 0.005 80)

## Anti-references
- The dark mode Vercel/Linear clone (too techy, too expected)
- Notion (too document-ey)
- Any SaaS with gradient text headings and purple glows
- Generic "AI productivity app" with robot icons and blue digital effects
- Indistinguishable card grids with gray borders

## Strategic Principles
1. Every pixel should feel like it was placed with intent
2. Embrace negative space — "airy" means room to breathe
3. Animations provide analog/physical feedback (exponential ease-outs, no bouncy elasticity)
4. Use typographic scale and whitespace instead of boxed cards for structure
5. Copy earns its place — every word, every line

## CTA Goal
Email waitlist capture — "Get early access" — feeds into Make.com → Google Sheets + welcome email
